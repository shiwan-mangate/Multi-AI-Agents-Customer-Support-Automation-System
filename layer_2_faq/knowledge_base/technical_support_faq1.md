## SECTION 1 — GETTING STARTED WITH SUPPORT
---

**Q: How do I contact technical support?**  
A: You can reach our technical support team through the following channels:

- **Live Chat:** Available on the website and mobile app. Click the chat icon at the bottom right corner. Available 24/7 for critical issues and during business hours for general queries.
- **Email Support:** Submit a ticket via Support → Contact Us or email our support address directly. Response within 24–48 hours.
- **Phone Support:** Available for Pro and Enterprise plan users. The support number is listed in Account Settings → Support.
- **Help Center:** Browse self-service articles and troubleshooting guides at support.yourplatform.com.
- **Community Forum:** Peer-supported discussions for common questions and feature requests.

For critical or account-compromising issues, live chat or phone support is recommended for the fastest resolution.

**Q: What are your technical support hours?**  
A: Support availability depends on your plan:

| Plan | Support Hours |
|---|---|
| Free | Business hours (Mon–Fri, 9 AM – 6 PM IST) via chat and email |
| Starter | Business hours (Mon–Fri, 9 AM – 9 PM IST) via chat and email |
| Pro | Extended hours (Mon–Sat, 8 AM – 11 PM IST) via chat, email, and phone |
| Enterprise | 24/7 support via dedicated channel, phone, and email |

During non-business hours, you can use the Help Center for self-service or submit an email ticket that will be picked up when support resumes. Critical system outages are monitored 24/7 regardless of plan.

**Q: How do I submit a support ticket?**  
A: To submit a support ticket:

1. Log in to your account and go to Support → New Ticket.
2. Select the issue category (e.g., Account, Billing, Technical, Feature Request).
3. Provide a clear subject line and a detailed description of the issue.
4. Attach screenshots, error messages, screen recordings, or log files if relevant.
5. Click "Submit." You will receive a ticket reference number and an email confirmation.

You can track ticket status and reply to the assigned agent from Support → My Tickets. Providing detailed information upfront reduces back-and-forth and speeds up resolution.

**Q: What information should I include when reporting a technical issue?**  
A: The more detail you provide, the faster we can resolve your issue. Always include:

- **Description of the problem:** What happened, what you expected to happen, and what actually occurred
- **Steps to reproduce:** The exact sequence of actions that triggers the issue
- **Error messages:** The exact text or a screenshot of any error shown
- **Device details:** Device type, operating system, and version (e.g., iPhone 15, iOS 17.4)
- **Browser details:** Browser name and version if reporting a web issue (e.g., Chrome 124)
- **Account details:** Your registered email and the affected feature or page
- **When it started:** Approximate date and time the issue first occurred
- **Frequency:** Does it happen always, sometimes, or only under specific conditions?

If the issue involves data or a specific record, include the relevant ID or a screenshot.

**Q: How do I check the status of my support ticket?**  
A: Log in and go to Support → My Tickets. Each ticket shows its current status:

- **Open:** Ticket received and awaiting assignment
- **In Progress:** Assigned to an agent actively working on it
- **Awaiting Your Response:** Agent has replied and needs more information from you
- **Resolved:** Issue has been fixed or answered
- **Closed:** Ticket closed after resolution confirmation

You will receive email notifications for every status change. If a ticket is marked "Awaiting Your Response" and you do not reply within 7 days, it will be automatically closed. You can reopen it by replying to the ticket email.

**Q: What are the ticket priority levels and response times?**  
A: Tickets are assigned a priority level based on impact:

| Priority | Description | First Response Target |
|---|---|---|
| Critical (P1) | Complete service outage, data loss risk, security breach | Within 1 hour |
| High (P2) | Major feature broken, significant business impact | Within 4 hours |
| Medium (P3) | Feature partially broken, workaround available | Within 12 hours |
| Low (P4) | Minor issue, cosmetic bug, general question | Within 24–48 hours |

Enterprise customers get prioritized queuing across all levels. Response times are targets, not SLA guarantees, except for Enterprise plan customers with a signed SLA.

## SECTION 2 — ACCOUNT & LOGIN ISSUES
---

**Q: I cannot log in to my account. What should I do?**  
A: Follow these steps to troubleshoot login issues:

1. **Check your credentials:** Ensure you are using the correct email and password. Passwords are case-sensitive.
2. **Reset your password:** Click "Forgot Password?" on the login page and follow the instructions.
3. **Check for account lock:** If you see a message about too many failed attempts, wait 15–30 minutes before trying again.
4. **Try a different browser or incognito mode:** This rules out browser extension or cache conflicts.
5. **Clear browser cache and cookies:** Stale cache can interfere with login sessions.
6. **Check your email for security alerts:** We may have locked your account due to suspicious activity.
7. **Check service status:** Visit our Status Page to see if there is an ongoing login service disruption.

If none of these steps work, contact support with your registered email address.

**Q: I am not receiving the password reset email. What should I do?**  
A: If the password reset email has not arrived:

1. **Check spam and junk folders:** Automated emails sometimes get filtered.
2. **Wait a few minutes:** Delivery can take up to 5 minutes during peak periods.
3. **Verify the email address:** Ensure you entered the exact email registered to your account.
4. **Check email filters:** A corporate firewall or email filter may be blocking the message.
5. **Try adding our domain to your safe sender list** and request a new reset email.
6. **Try an alternative email client:** If using webmail (e.g., Gmail), try accessing it from a different device.

If the issue persists, contact support and our team can verify your identity through alternative means and assist with account recovery.

**Q: I lost access to my two-factor authentication (2FA) device. How do I recover my account?**  
A: If you cannot access your 2FA device:

1. **Use a backup code:** During 2FA setup, backup codes were provided. Enter one of these codes on the 2FA screen.
2. **Use the recovery option:** On the 2FA screen, click "Can't access your device?" and follow the recovery flow.
3. **Contact support:** If you have no backup codes, contact our support team. Account recovery without 2FA requires strict identity verification, which may include submitting a government-issued ID and answering security questions. This process can take 24–72 hours to protect account integrity.

After recovering your account, immediately set up a new 2FA method and store backup codes in a secure location.

**Q: My account has been suspended. What does this mean and what should I do?**  
A: Account suspension means your account has been temporarily disabled. Common reasons include:

- Suspicious or fraudulent activity detected
- Payment failure not resolved within the grace period
- Violation of our Terms of Service
- Security hold placed by our team pending investigation

You will receive an email explaining the reason for suspension. To resolve it:

- For **billing-related suspension:** Update your payment method in Billing Settings and the account will be automatically reactivated.
- For **policy violation or security-related suspension:** Reply to the suspension email or contact support to initiate a review.
- If you believe the suspension was made in error, submit an appeal via Support → Account Appeal.

**Q: My account shows activity I did not perform. What should I do?**  
A: If you notice unfamiliar activity on your account, act immediately:

1. **Change your password** from Account Settings → Security → Change Password.
2. **Enable 2FA** if not already active.
3. **Log out of all active sessions** from Account Settings → Security → Active Sessions → Log Out All.
4. **Review recent activity** under Account Settings → Security → Login History.
5. **Contact support** and report the suspicious activity. Our security team will investigate and can freeze the account while the review is in progress.
6. **Check connected apps** for any unauthorized third-party integrations under Account Settings → Integrations.

Do not delay — unauthorized access can escalate quickly.

## SECTION 3 — APP & PLATFORM ISSUES
---

**Q: The platform is not loading or is very slow. What should I do?**  
A: Try these steps to resolve loading or performance issues:

1. **Check the Status Page:** Visit status.yourplatform.com to see if there is an active outage or degraded performance notice.
2. **Refresh the page:** Press Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac) for a hard refresh.
3. **Clear browser cache:** Go to browser settings and clear cached images and files.
4. **Try a different browser:** Chrome, Firefox, and Edge are fully supported. Safari may have occasional compatibility issues.
5. **Disable browser extensions:** Ad blockers and script blockers can interfere with platform loading. Try in incognito/private mode.
6. **Check your internet connection:** Run a speed test to ensure your connection is stable.
7. **Try a different network:** Switch from Wi-Fi to mobile data or vice versa to rule out network-specific issues.

If the problem persists on multiple browsers and networks, contact support with a screenshot of any error messages.

**Q: I am getting an error message. How do I report it?**  
A: To report an error message effectively:

1. **Screenshot or copy the exact error text.** Include the error code if one is displayed (e.g., Error 403, Error 500).
2. **Note what you were doing** when the error appeared — the page, the action, and the sequence of steps.
3. **Check the Help Center** for the specific error code. Common error codes and their meanings are documented in our Error Code Reference guide.
4. **Submit a support ticket** via Support → New Ticket, attaching the screenshot and description.

For reference, common HTTP error codes:

| Error Code | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 400 | Bad Request | Check inputs and retry |
| 401 | Unauthorized | Log out and log back in |
| 403 | Forbidden | Check permissions or contact admin |
| 404 | Not Found | Page or resource may have moved |
| 429 | Too Many Requests | Wait a few minutes and retry |
| 500 | Internal Server Error | Contact support with details |
| 503 | Service Unavailable | Check Status Page for outage |

**Q: A feature I was using is suddenly not working. What should I do?**  
A: If a previously working feature has stopped functioning:

1. **Check the Status Page** for any announced maintenance or feature-specific outages.
2. **Check the Changelog or Announcements** — the feature may have been updated or temporarily disabled.
3. **Clear cache and try again** — sometimes outdated cached data causes features to break.
4. **Try a different browser or device** to rule out a local environment issue.
5. **Check your plan** — if you recently changed plans, the feature may no longer be included.
6. **Log out and log back in** to refresh your session and permissions.

If none of these help, submit a support ticket describing the feature, last time it worked, and the current behavior.

**Q: The mobile app is crashing or freezing. How do I fix it?**  
A: Try the following steps to resolve app crashes or freezes:

1. **Force close the app** and reopen it.
2. **Restart your device** — this clears temporary memory issues.
3. **Check for app updates:** Go to the App Store (iOS) or Play Store (Android) and update to the latest version.
4. **Check device storage:** Low storage (less than 1 GB free) can cause apps to crash. Free up space if needed.
5. **Clear the app cache:** On Android, go to Settings → Apps → Your App → Clear Cache. On iOS, offload and reinstall the app.
6. **Reinstall the app:** Uninstall and reinstall the app. This resolves issues caused by corrupted app files.
7. **Check OS compatibility:** Ensure your device OS meets our minimum requirements (iOS 14+ or Android 9+).

If the app continues to crash after reinstalling, contact support with your device model, OS version, and a description of when the crash occurs.

**Q: I am having trouble with a specific browser. Which browsers do you support?**  
A: We officially support the following browsers and versions:

| Browser | Minimum Version | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Google Chrome | Version 110+ | Recommended |
| Mozilla Firefox | Version 110+ | Fully supported |
| Microsoft Edge | Version 110+ | Fully supported |
| Safari | Version 15+ | Mostly supported; minor UI differences |
| Opera | Version 95+ | Supported |
| Internet Explorer | Any version | Not supported |

For the best experience, use the latest version of Chrome or Firefox. If you are experiencing browser-specific issues, try Chrome first as it is our primary development and testing environment. Browser extensions, especially ad blockers or script managers, can sometimes conflict with platform features — test in incognito mode to rule this out.

**Q: I cannot upload files on the platform. What might be wrong?**  
A: File upload issues can be caused by several factors. Check the following:

1. **File size limit:** Our platform supports uploads up to 50 MB per file. Files exceeding this limit will be rejected.
2. **File type restriction:** Accepted formats are listed on the upload area. Unsupported formats (e.g., .exe, .bat, .sh) are blocked for security reasons.
3. **Browser permissions:** Ensure the browser has permission to access files. Some security policies in corporate environments restrict file access.
4. **Internet speed:** Large file uploads require a stable connection. A drop mid-upload will fail the upload.
5. **Antivirus or firewall:** Some security software intercepts file uploads. Try temporarily disabling and testing.
6. **Storage quota:** Check if your account has reached its storage limit under Account Settings → Storage.
7. **Clear browser cache** and retry the upload.

If the issue persists, try uploading from a different browser or device and contact support with the file type and size.

**Q: Notifications are not working. How do I fix this?**  
A: If you are not receiving in-app, email, or push notifications:

**For email notifications:**
- Check your spam or junk folder.
- Verify your email address is confirmed in Account Settings → Profile.
- Review notification settings under Account Settings → Notifications.

**For push notifications (mobile app):**
- Ensure push notifications are enabled for the app in your device settings (Settings → Notifications → App Name).
- Check that Do Not Disturb mode is not active.
- Log out and log back in to refresh the notification token.

**For in-app notifications:**
- Refresh the page or restart the app.
- Check notification filters — you may have muted certain notification types.

If none of these steps resolve the issue, contact support with your device OS, app version, and the type of notification you are missing.

## SECTION 4 — INTEGRATIONS & API ISSUES
---

**Q: My third-party integration has stopped working. What should I do?**  
A: Third-party integration failures are often caused by authentication token expiry, API changes, or configuration issues. Try the following:

1. **Re-authenticate the integration:** Go to Account Settings → Integrations → Find the integration → Disconnect and reconnect. This refreshes the access tokens.
2. **Check the integration's own status:** The third-party service may be experiencing an outage. Check their status page.
3. **Review API permissions:** Ensure the permissions granted during setup still match what the integration requires.
4. **Check for API version changes:** If we recently updated our API, the integration may need to be updated. Review the API Changelog in our developer documentation.
5. **Test with a simple API call:** Use our API testing tool or a tool like Postman to verify basic connectivity.

If the issue is on our end, check our Status Page for any API-related incidents. Contact developer support if the problem persists.

**Q: I am getting API authentication errors. How do I resolve them?**  
A: API authentication errors (typically HTTP 401 Unauthorized or 403 Forbidden) are caused by the following:

- **Expired API key:** API keys may expire based on your security policy. Regenerate a new key from Account Settings → API → Manage Keys.
- **Incorrect API key format:** Ensure the key is passed correctly in the request header as `Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY`.
- **Wrong environment:** Ensure you are using the correct key for the environment (production vs. sandbox). Keys are not interchangeable.
- **IP restrictions:** If you configured IP whitelisting on the API key, requests from non-whitelisted IPs will be rejected.
- **Insufficient permissions:** The API key may not have the scopes required for the endpoint you are calling. Check the key's permission scopes.
- **Rate limiting:** If you are making too many requests in a short period, you may be temporarily blocked (HTTP 429). Implement exponential backoff.

Consult our API documentation at docs.yourplatform.com for endpoint-specific authentication requirements.

**Q: What are the API rate limits and how do I handle them?**  
A: API rate limits are enforced per API key to ensure fair usage:

| Plan | Rate Limit |
|---|---|
| Free | 100 requests per minute |
| Starter | 500 requests per minute |
| Pro | 2,000 requests per minute |
| Enterprise | Custom limits (negotiated) |

When you exceed the rate limit, the API returns an HTTP 429 Too Many Requests response with a `Retry-After` header indicating how long to wait. Best practices to handle rate limits:

- Implement exponential backoff with jitter in your retry logic.
- Cache responses where possible to reduce redundant requests.
- Use batch endpoints where available to combine multiple operations.
- Monitor your usage via the API Dashboard under Developer Settings.

Contact enterprise support if your use case requires higher limits.

**Q: How do I generate or rotate an API key?**  
A: To generate a new API key:

1. Go to Account Settings → Developer → API Keys.
2. Click "Generate New Key."
3. Assign a name (e.g., "Production App"), select permission scopes, and optionally set an expiry date.
4. Copy and store the key securely — it will only be shown once.

To rotate (replace) an existing key:
1. Select the key you want to rotate.
2. Click "Rotate Key." A new key is generated and the old key is invalidated after a 24-hour grace period (configurable).
3. Update your application with the new key before the grace period ends.

Never commit API keys to source code repositories. Use environment variables or secrets managers.

**Q: Where can I find API documentation and code samples?**  
A: Complete API documentation is available at docs.yourplatform.com/api. It includes:

- **API Reference:** All endpoints, request parameters, response formats, and error codes
- **Authentication Guide:** How to authenticate using API keys and OAuth 2.0
- **Quickstart Guides:** Getting started tutorials for common use cases
- **Code Samples:** Examples in Python, JavaScript (Node.js), Java, PHP, Ruby, and cURL
- **Changelog:** History of API version changes and deprecation notices
- **Sandbox Environment:** A test environment where you can make live API calls without affecting production data

If you are looking for SDKs, official libraries are available on our GitHub repository. Community-contributed SDKs for additional languages are also listed in the documentation.

## SECTION 5 — PERFORMANCE & CONNECTIVITY ISSUES
---

**Q: The platform is running slowly for me. How do I diagnose the issue?**  
A: To diagnose slow platform performance:

1. **Run a speed test** at speedtest.net to check your internet connection speed. We recommend at least 5 Mbps for standard use and 25 Mbps for media-heavy features.
2. **Check the Status Page** for any ongoing performance degradation incidents.
3. **Test on a different network:** Switch from Wi-Fi to mobile data or try a different Wi-Fi network to isolate network-specific issues.
4. **Check browser performance:** Open browser Developer Tools (F12) → Network tab → Reload the page. Look for requests taking more than 3 seconds.
5. **Close other browser tabs and apps** that may be consuming bandwidth or memory.
6. **Try a different device** to determine if the issue is device-specific.
7. **Check for background updates** on your device that may be consuming bandwidth.

Share findings from the Network tab when contacting support — it helps engineers pinpoint whether the slowness is on your device, your network, or our servers.

**Q: I keep getting disconnected or timed out. What can I do?**  
A: Frequent disconnections or session timeouts are usually network or session-related. Try the following:

1. **Check your internet stability:** Use a wired connection instead of Wi-Fi if possible, as Wi-Fi can drop intermittently.
2. **Review session timeout settings:** Your account may be configured to time out after a period of inactivity. Adjust this under Account Settings → Security → Session Timeout.
3. **Disable VPN temporarily:** VPNs can cause unstable connections to our servers depending on the VPN server location.
4. **Check for proxy interference:** Corporate proxies sometimes interrupt long-lived connections. Contact your IT department.
5. **Clear cookies and site data:** Corrupted session cookies can cause repeated logouts.
6. **Check browser settings:** Some privacy-focused browser settings automatically clear cookies on tab close.

If timeouts happen during active use (not idle), escalate to support with the time of occurrence and your approximate geographic location.

**Q: I am behind a corporate firewall and cannot access the platform. What should I do?**  
A: Corporate firewalls and network proxies can block access to our platform. Share the following information with your IT department to whitelist our services:

- **Domains to whitelist:**
  - `yourplatform.com` and all subdomains (`*.yourplatform.com`)
  - `cdn.yourplatform.com` (media and assets)
  - `api.yourplatform.com` (API endpoints)
  - `status.yourplatform.com` (status page)
- **Ports to allow:** 443 (HTTPS), 80 (HTTP redirects)
- **WebSocket support:** Required for live chat and real-time features (WSS on port 443)
- **IP ranges:** Our current IP range list is available in the developer documentation for IT whitelisting purposes

If your organization uses a web content filter, ensure our platform is categorized appropriately. Contact our enterprise support team for an IT administrator guide tailored for corporate deployments.

## SECTION 6 — DATA SYNC & IMPORT/EXPORT ISSUES
---

**Q: My data is not syncing across devices. How do I fix it?**  
A: Data sync issues across devices are typically caused by connectivity problems or sync conflicts. Try the following:

1. **Check internet connection** on both devices — sync requires an active connection.
2. **Force a manual sync:** On mobile, pull down on the main screen to trigger a refresh. On web, hard refresh the page (Ctrl+Shift+R).
3. **Log out and log back in** on the affected device to re-establish the sync session.
4. **Check the sync status indicator** in the app — a warning icon may indicate a sync error with a description.
5. **Ensure both devices are running the latest app version** — version mismatches can cause sync conflicts.
6. **Check if the data was saved:** On the originating device, confirm the data was actually saved (look for "Saving..." or "Saved" confirmation).

If data is still out of sync after these steps, contact support with the device types, versions, and a description of what data is missing.

**Q: My data import failed. What are the common reasons and how do I fix them?**  
A: Data import failures are most commonly caused by:

- **Incorrect file format:** Ensure your file is in the accepted format (CSV, XLSX, or JSON depending on the import type). Download our import template from the import wizard for the correct structure.
- **Column header mismatch:** Column names in your file must match our expected headers exactly. Extra columns are ignored; missing required columns cause failure.
- **Data formatting errors:** Date fields must follow YYYY-MM-DD format. Phone numbers must include country code. Blank required fields cause row-level errors.
- **File encoding:** Use UTF-8 encoding. Files saved with other encodings (e.g., UTF-16, Latin-1) may import incorrectly.
- **File size limit:** Imports are limited to 10,000 rows or 20 MB per file. Split larger files into batches.
- **Duplicate records:** If the platform detects duplicate entries based on a unique key (e.g., email), the import may halt or skip those rows depending on your duplicate handling setting.

After a failed import, download the error report from the Import History page. It lists each row that failed and the specific reason, so you can fix and re-import.

**Q: How do I export my data from the platform?**  
A: To export your data:

1. Go to the relevant section (e.g., Contacts, Orders, Reports).
2. Click the "Export" button, usually located in the top right area of the list view.
3. Select the data range (date range, filters) and export format (CSV, XLSX, PDF where applicable).
4. Click "Export." For large data sets, the export is processed in the background and a download link is sent to your email.

You can also initiate a full account data export from Account Settings → Privacy → Export My Data for a comprehensive archive of all your data. Note that large exports may take up to 30 minutes to prepare.

**Q: I accidentally deleted data. Can it be recovered?**  
A: Recovery depends on what was deleted and when:

- **Items in Trash / Recycle Bin:** Most deletable items are moved to a Trash folder first. Go to the relevant section → Trash and restore within 30 days.
- **Permanently deleted items:** Items permanently deleted (emptied from Trash) may be recoverable from our server-side backups within 7 days of deletion. Contact support immediately with the item type, approximate deletion time, and the record ID if available.
- **After 7 days:** Permanent deletions older than 7 days are not recoverable from backups. This is why we recommend regular exports for critical data.

For bulk accidental deletions, contact support urgently — the sooner you raise the request, the higher the chance of recovery.

## SECTION 7 — BILLING & SUBSCRIPTION TECHNICAL ISSUES
---

**Q: My payment is not going through. What technical steps should I try?**  
A: If a payment attempt is failing technically (not due to insufficient funds), try the following:

1. **Verify card details:** Double-check the card number, expiry date, CVV, and billing address ZIP/postal code.
2. **Enable international transactions:** If the charge is originating from a country different from where your card was issued, your bank may block it. Contact your bank to enable international charges.
3. **Try a different browser:** Occasionally, browser extensions or autofill issues interfere with payment forms.
4. **Disable VPN:** Some payment processors block transactions from VPN-masked IP addresses.
5. **Try a different card or payment method:** Rule out card-specific issues by using an alternative payment method.
6. **Check for 3D Secure prompts:** Some banks require additional authentication (OTP) for online payments. Ensure pop-ups are not being blocked during checkout.
7. **Clear browser cache and retry.**

If none of these steps work, contact support with the error message shown at checkout (do not share full card details).

**Q: I was charged but my plan was not upgraded. What should I do?**  
A: If payment was successful but the upgrade did not reflect:

1. **Wait a few minutes and hard refresh** the page. Plan changes can take 2–3 minutes to propagate.
2. **Log out and log back in** to refresh your account permissions and plan data.
3. **Check your email** for a payment confirmation and order confirmation for the upgrade.
4. **Verify in Billing Settings:** Go to Account Settings → Billing → Subscription. It should reflect the new plan.

If the charge appears on your bank statement but the plan is still not upgraded after 10 minutes, contact support immediately with the transaction ID or last 4 digits of the card used. We will investigate and either apply the upgrade or issue a full refund promptly.

## SECTION 8 — EMAIL & COMMUNICATION ISSUES
---

**Q: I am not receiving emails from your platform. How do I fix this?**  
A: If platform emails (receipts, alerts, password resets) are not arriving:

1. **Check your spam/junk folder** — automated emails are sometimes filtered.
2. **Add our sender address to your safe sender or contacts list** to prevent future filtering.
3. **Verify your email address** in Account Settings → Profile. If it is unverified, transactional emails may not be sent.
4. **Check email forwarding rules** — if you have set up email filters or forwarding, messages may be redirected or deleted.
5. **Check with your email provider or IT team** — corporate email systems sometimes block domains they deem as bulk senders.
6. **Test a different email address:** Add a secondary email in your account settings and see if emails reach that address.

Our email sending domains are listed in the Help Center. Ask your IT team to whitelist them if you are using a corporate email system.

**Q: I am receiving too many email notifications. How do I reduce them?**  
A: You can granularly control all email notifications from Account Settings → Notifications → Email Preferences. From there you can:

- Turn off individual notification types (activity summaries, comment mentions, etc.)
- Change frequency of digest emails (real-time, daily, weekly)
- Unsubscribe from non-essential notification categories entirely

Essential transactional emails (security alerts, payment receipts, password resets) cannot be disabled. If you are receiving duplicate emails, this may indicate you have multiple accounts registered to the same address — contact support to investigate.

## SECTION 10 — ESCALATION & ADVANCED SUPPORT
---

**Q: My issue has not been resolved after multiple tickets. How do I escalate?**  
A: If your issue remains unresolved after reasonable attempts, use the following escalation path:

1. **Reply to your existing ticket** and explicitly request escalation to a senior support engineer. Provide a summary of all prior steps and resolutions attempted.
2. **Use the Escalation Request form** under Support → Escalate Issue. Include your ticket reference numbers.
3. **Contact the Support Manager:** Pro and Enterprise customers can request direct contact with the Support Manager. Ask your assigned agent for the contact.
4. **Enterprise customers:** Use the dedicated escalation channel in your customer success portal or contact your Customer Success Manager (CSM) directly.

**Q: How do I set up a sandbox or test environment?**  
A: A sandbox environment is available for all paid plan users to safely test integrations, APIs, and workflows without affecting production data. To set it up:

1. Go to Account Settings → Developer → Sandbox Environment.
2. Click "Enable Sandbox." A separate sandbox account will be created linked to your primary account.
3. Access the sandbox via sandbox.yourplatform.com using your existing credentials.
4. Use sandbox-specific API keys (available under Developer → API Keys → Sandbox) for test API calls.

The sandbox mirrors production features but uses isolated test data. Payment processing in the sandbox uses test card numbers provided in our developer documentation.

**Q: How do I enable and use webhooks?**  
A: Webhooks allow our platform to send real-time event notifications to your server. To configure:

1. Go to Account Settings → Developer → Webhooks → Add Endpoint.
2. Enter your endpoint URL (must be HTTPS) and select the events you want to subscribe to.
3. Copy the generated webhook secret — use this to verify the signature of incoming webhook payloads.
4. Click "Save." We will send a test ping to your endpoint to verify connectivity.

Each webhook payload includes a signature header (`X-Platform-Signature`) containing an HMAC-SHA256 hash. Verify this signature on your server before processing the payload. Webhook delivery is retried up to 5 times with exponential backoff if your endpoint does not return HTTP 200.

**Q: How do I troubleshoot webhook delivery failures?**  
A: To diagnose webhook issues:

1. **Check Webhook Logs:** Go to Developer → Webhooks → Delivery Logs. Each delivery attempt is logged with the request payload, response code, and response body.
2. **Verify endpoint availability:** Ensure your server is publicly accessible and returns HTTP 200 for successful receipts. Redirects (3xx) are not followed.
3. **Check response time:** Webhook endpoints must respond within 5 seconds. If your server takes longer, the delivery is marked as failed even if processing completes later. Use asynchronous processing — accept the webhook immediately and process in a background job.
4. **Validate signature verification:** If your verification logic is incorrect, it may reject valid webhooks. Test signature verification with a known test payload.
5. **Check server logs** for any errors during payload processing.

You can manually retry a failed delivery from the Delivery Logs page.

**Q: I am seeing CORS errors when making API calls from my frontend. How do I fix them?**  
A: CORS (Cross-Origin Resource Sharing) errors occur when API calls are made from a browser to our API from a domain not in our allowed origins list. The recommended approach is to **never make direct API calls from a frontend browser application using your secret API key** — this exposes your key. Instead:

1. **Use a backend proxy:** Route API calls through your own backend server, which adds the API key server-side and returns the response to your frontend.
2. **Use our JavaScript SDK:** Our official JS SDK handles CORS correctly and uses a browser-safe authentication mechanism.
3. **For OAuth flows:** If building a frontend app with user authentication, use our OAuth 2.0 authorization code flow which returns tokens scoped to the logged-in user.

If you are a developer building a server-side integration, CORS does not apply. Consult our developer documentation for the appropriate authentication pattern for your architecture.

## SECTION 13 — FREQUENTLY ENCOUNTERED TECHNICAL SCENARIOS

**Q: I see a "Session Expired" message. What should I do?**  
A: A "Session Expired" message means your login session has timed out due to inactivity or a security event. Simply log in again. If you were actively using the platform and the session expired unexpectedly, check the following:

- Your session timeout setting (Account Settings → Security → Session Timeout) — it may be set to a short duration.
- Your browser settings may be clearing cookies or blocking them, ending sessions prematurely.
- A simultaneous login from another device can sometimes invalidate your current session depending on your security settings.

If the session expires repeatedly during active work, contact support as it may indicate a token management issue on our end.

**Q: I see a "403 Forbidden" error when accessing a page. What does it mean?**  
A: A 403 error means you do not have permission to access the resource you requested. Common causes:

- **Insufficient plan:** The feature or page requires a higher subscription tier.
- **Role restriction:** Your account role (e.g., Viewer) does not have access to that section. Ask an Admin to update your role.
- **Workspace restriction:** An admin has restricted access to certain features for your user group.
- **Expired trial:** If your trial ended, pages that required a paid plan will now return 403.
- **Geo-restriction:** Certain features may not be available in your region.

Check your plan and role first. If you believe you should have access but are getting 403, contact support with the URL and your account email.

**Q: Pages are loading with broken layout or missing images. How do I fix this?**  
A: Broken layouts or missing images are usually caused by:

1. **Cached stale assets:** Hard refresh the page (Ctrl+Shift+R / Cmd+Shift+R) to force reload of CSS and images.
2. **Ad blocker or content blocker:** These can block our CDN assets. Test in incognito mode without extensions.
3. **Browser zoom level:** Unusual zoom levels (above 150%) can cause layout shifts. Reset to 100% with Ctrl+0.
4. **Partially loaded page:** If your connection dropped mid-load, some assets may not have loaded. Refresh the page.
5. **Browser cache corruption:** Clear the browser cache entirely (Settings → Clear browsing data → Cached images and files).
6. **CDN issue:** If the issue affects many users, our CDN may be having problems. Check the Status Page.

If the broken layout persists across browsers and devices, it may be a platform bug. Take a screenshot and report it to support.

**Q: I submitted a form but nothing happened. What should I do?**  
A: If a form submission appears to have no effect, try the following:

1. **Check for validation errors:** Scroll up on the form — error messages sometimes appear at the top or inline next to specific fields.
2. **Check required fields:** Ensure all mandatory fields (usually marked with an asterisk) are filled in.
3. **Check your internet connection:** The form may not have submitted if your connection dropped.
4. **Disable browser extensions:** Some security extensions block form submissions. Try in incognito mode.
5. **Try a different browser:** Reproducing on another browser helps confirm if it is a browser-specific bug.
6. **Check the browser console:** Press F12 → Console tab and look for red error messages. Screenshot these and include them in a support ticket.

If the form consistently does not submit, this is likely a bug. Report it with the form name, URL, and any console errors.

**Q: I received a "Quota Exceeded" error. What does it mean?**  
A: A "Quota Exceeded" error means you have reached a usage limit imposed by your current plan. Common quotas include:

| Quota Type | Where to Check |
|---|---|
| Storage space | Account Settings → Storage |
| Number of projects / workspaces | Account Settings → Plan |
| API request volume | Developer → API Dashboard |
| Number of team members | Account Settings → Team |
| Email sends per month | Account Settings → Usage |

To resolve, either upgrade your plan to a higher tier with increased limits, or remove unused items to free up quota. Go to Account Settings → Billing → Upgrade Plan to view available options.

**Q: I think I found a bug. How do I report it responsibly?**  
A: We appreciate bug reports from our users. To report a bug:

1. **Check the Status Page and Known Issues** section to see if it is already acknowledged.
2. **Reproduce the bug** and document the exact steps, expected behavior, and actual behavior.
3. **Collect evidence:** Screenshots, screen recordings, browser console logs (F12 → Console), and network logs help engineers investigate faster.
4. **Submit a support ticket** via Support → New Ticket → Category: Bug Report. Include all collected evidence.

For **security vulnerabilities** (e.g., data exposure, authentication bypass), do not submit through the regular ticket system. Instead, report via our responsible disclosure channel listed in our Security Policy — we have a bug bounty program that may reward impactful security findings.
