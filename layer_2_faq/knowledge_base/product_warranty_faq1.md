## SECTION 1 — WARRANTY BASICS
---

**Q: What is a product warranty?**  
A: A product warranty is a formal commitment by the manufacturer or seller that a product will function as described for a specified period of time. If the product fails due to defects in materials or workmanship within the warranty period, the manufacturer will repair it, replace it, or issue a refund — depending on the terms. A warranty gives you legal protection and assurance about the quality of your purchase.

**Q: What types of warranties do you offer?**  
A: We offer the following types of warranties:

| Warranty Type | Coverage | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard Warranty** | Manufacturing defects, hardware failure | 1 year from purchase |
| **Extended Warranty** | All standard coverage + accidental damage | 2–3 years (purchasable) |
| **Limited Lifetime Warranty** | Specific components (e.g., frame, chassis) | Lifetime of product |
| **Onsite Warranty** | Technician visits your location for repair | 1–2 years (select products) |

Specific warranty terms vary by product category. Refer to the product page or the warranty card in the box for exact terms.

**Q: How long is the warranty period for my product?**  
A: Warranty duration depends on the product category:

- **Electronics (laptops, phones, tablets):** 1 year standard
- **Home appliances:** 1–2 years standard (with optional extension)
- **Peripherals and accessories:** 6 months to 1 year
- **Furniture and fixtures:** 2–5 years depending on material
- **Industrial/commercial equipment:** 1–3 years

The exact warranty start and end date is mentioned on your warranty card and in your purchase invoice. You can also check your warranty status by logging into your account and visiting Orders → Warranty Status.

**Q: When does the warranty period start?**  
A: The warranty period begins on the **date of purchase** as recorded on your original invoice or receipt. For products purchased online, the start date is the delivery date confirmed by the courier. For gifted products, the warranty still starts from the original purchase date, not the date of gifting.

**Q: Does my product come with a warranty card?**  
A: Yes. Most products include a physical warranty card inside the packaging. The card contains the product serial number, warranty period, and instructions for claiming warranty service. If a warranty card is missing from the box, you can use your purchase invoice as proof of warranty. Contact customer support to register your product and get a digital warranty certificate.

**Q: Do I need to register my product to activate the warranty?**  
A: For most products, the warranty is automatically active from the purchase date and does not require separate registration. However, registering your product on our portal offers additional benefits such as faster claim processing, extended warranty offers, and maintenance reminders. To register, log in and go to My Products → Register New Product and enter the serial number from your product.

## SECTION 2 — WHAT THE WARRANTY COVERS
---

**Q: What does the standard warranty cover?**  
A: The standard warranty covers defects that arise from manufacturing faults or failures in materials under normal use conditions. This includes:

- Hardware component failures (e.g., screen malfunction, battery defect, motor failure)
- Electrical faults not caused by the user
- Structural defects in the product body or frame
- Pre-installed software or firmware issues (for electronics)
- Dead pixels beyond the acceptable threshold on display products

Coverage applies only when the product is used in accordance with the provided user manual and safety guidelines.

**Q: Are accessories and bundled items covered under warranty?**  
A: Accessories bundled with the main product (e.g., charging cables, remote controls, power adapters) carry a separate warranty period, typically 6 months. Third-party accessories purchased separately are not covered under our warranty. If a bundled accessory fails within its warranty period, you can raise a separate claim for it alongside or independent of the main product claim.

**Q: Is software covered under the product warranty?**  
A: Pre-installed software and firmware that comes with the product is covered for defects during the warranty period. If a software bug or firmware issue causes the product to malfunction, it will be addressed under warranty service. However, third-party applications, user-installed software, and operating system upgrades done by the user are not covered. Data loss due to software issues is also not covered under the standard warranty.

**Q: Is the battery covered under warranty?**  
A: Yes, batteries are covered under the standard warranty for manufacturing defects. A battery that fails to hold charge, does not charge at all, or swells within the warranty period due to a defect is eligible for replacement. However, normal battery degradation over time (capacity reduction with charge cycles) is considered wear and tear and is not covered. Batteries are typically covered for 6 months to 1 year depending on the product.

**Q: Are consumable parts covered under warranty?**  
A: Consumable and wear-and-tear components are generally not covered under the standard warranty. These include items like printer ink cartridges, filter pads, lamp bulbs, rubber seals, and similar parts that are designed to wear out with regular use. However, if a consumable fails prematurely due to a manufacturing defect within an unusually short time, it may be evaluated for warranty coverage on a case-by-case basis.

## SECTION 3 — WHAT THE WARRANTY DOES NOT COVER
---

**Q: What is NOT covered under the warranty?**  
A: The following are explicitly excluded from warranty coverage:

- **Physical damage:** Cracks, dents, scratches, broken screens from drops or impacts
- **Water or liquid damage:** Exposure to moisture, spills, flooding, or submersion beyond the product's rated IP level
- **Electrical damage:** Damage from power surges, incorrect voltage, or use with incompatible accessories
- **Unauthorized modifications:** Rooting, jailbreaking, firmware tampering, or unauthorized hardware modifications
- **User error:** Misuse, negligence, or operation outside the guidelines in the user manual
- **Normal wear and tear:** Cosmetic degradation, paint fading, minor scratches from regular use
- **Loss or theft:** The warranty does not cover lost or stolen products
- **Environmental damage:** Damage from fire, flood, lightning, earthquakes, or other natural disasters
- **Expired warranty:** Products outside the warranty period are not eligible for warranty service

**Q: Does physical damage void the entire warranty?**  
A: Physical damage to one part of the product does not necessarily void the warranty for unrelated components. For example, if the outer casing is cracked due to a drop but the internal components fail due to a manufacturing defect unrelated to the drop, the internal defect may still be covered. Each claim is evaluated by our service team. However, if it is determined that the physical damage contributed to the internal failure, the claim may be denied.

**Q: Does water damage void the warranty?**  
A: Yes, in most cases. Liquid or moisture ingress that causes damage will void the warranty unless the product is rated for the level of water exposure involved (e.g., an IP67-rated product used at the specified depth). Products with activated Liquid Contact Indicators (LCIs) will be identified as having been exposed to liquid, and warranty coverage will be denied for that damage. Some extended warranty or protection plans do cover accidental liquid damage.

**Q: Will unauthorized repair or modification void my warranty?**  
A: Yes. Any repair, modification, or tampering performed by an unauthorized service center or individual will void the warranty immediately. This includes opening the product casing without authorization, replacing components with non-OEM parts, flashing unofficial firmware, or rooting/jailbreaking software-controlled devices. Always use our authorized service network to maintain your warranty coverage.

**Q: Is cosmetic damage covered under warranty?**  
A: No. Cosmetic damage such as scratches, scuffs, paint chipping, fading, or dents that do not affect the product's functionality are not covered under the standard warranty. These are considered normal wear and tear from regular use. If you want coverage for cosmetic damage, consider purchasing an extended protection plan that includes cosmetic coverage.

## SECTION 4 — MAKING A WARRANTY CLAIM
---

**Q: How do I file a warranty claim?**  
A: To file a warranty claim, follow these steps:

1. **Gather your documents:** Purchase invoice or receipt, product serial number, and a description of the issue.
2. **Log in to your account** and go to Orders → Select the product → Raise Warranty Claim.
3. **Describe the defect** in detail and upload photos or videos of the issue if applicable.
4. **Choose a service option:** Carry-in service, doorstep pickup, or onsite visit (availability varies by product and location).
5. **Submit the claim.** You will receive a claim reference number and an estimated resolution timeline via email.

Alternatively, you can call our warranty support helpline or visit an authorized service center directly.

**Q: What documents do I need to make a warranty claim?**  
A: To process a warranty claim, you will need:

- **Original purchase invoice or receipt** showing the product name, date of purchase, and purchase price
- **Product serial number** (found on the product, packaging, or warranty card)
- **Warranty card** (if applicable)
- **Photo or video evidence** of the defect (required for online claims)
- **Government-issued ID** (required for onsite service visits only)

If you purchased the product as a gift, the original buyer's invoice is still required. Duplicate invoices can be requested from the retailer or from your order history if purchased online.

**Q: How long does it take to process a warranty claim?**  
A: Typical processing timelines are as follows:

| Service Type | Estimated Timeline |
|---|---|
| Carry-in repair | 3–7 business days |
| Doorstep pickup & repair | 5–10 business days |
| Onsite service visit | Within 48 hours (scheduling dependent) |
| Replacement dispatch | 2–5 business days after claim approval |
| Refund processing | 7–10 business days after claim approval |

Timelines may vary based on parts availability, location, and claim complexity. You will receive real-time status updates via email and SMS.

**Q: Can I track the status of my warranty claim?**  
A: Yes. Log in to your account and go to Support → My Claims to see the real-time status of all your warranty claims. Each claim will show its current stage: Submitted, Under Review, Approved, In Repair, Dispatched, or Resolved. You will also receive automated email and SMS notifications whenever the status changes.

**Q: What happens after my warranty claim is approved?**  
A: Once a warranty claim is approved, one of the following resolutions will be offered based on the nature of the defect and product availability:

- **Repair:** The defective unit or component is repaired using genuine parts at no charge.
- **Replacement:** If the product cannot be repaired or the repair cost exceeds the product value, a replacement unit of the same model or equivalent will be dispatched.
- **Refund:** In rare cases where neither repair nor replacement is feasible, a refund equivalent to the product's current market value (not necessarily the original purchase price) may be issued.

The resolution type is at the discretion of the service team based on availability and feasibility.

**Q: My warranty claim was rejected. What can I do?**  
A: If your claim is rejected, you will receive a detailed rejection reason via email. Common reasons include physical or liquid damage, expired warranty, or evidence of unauthorized modification. You have the option to:

1. **Appeal the decision** — Submit an appeal within 14 days of rejection with additional evidence or clarification via the claim portal.
2. **Opt for paid repair** — Our service center can still repair the product at standard out-of-warranty service charges.
3. **Escalate to support** — Contact our senior customer support team if you believe the rejection was in error.

**Q: Can I raise a warranty claim without the original invoice?**  
A: The original invoice is the primary proof of purchase required for warranty claims. However, if you have lost the invoice, you may be able to substitute it with:

- A bank or credit card statement showing the purchase transaction
- Order confirmation email from our website (for online purchases)
- Retailer records if the purchase was made at a physical store

Claims without any proof of purchase will be evaluated on a case-by-case basis, often using the product's manufacturing date to estimate warranty eligibility. Contact warranty support to discuss your specific situation.

## SECTION 5 — WARRANTY SERVICE OPTIONS
---

**Q: What are the different warranty service modes available?**  
A: We offer three service modes depending on your product type and location:

- **Carry-in Service:** You bring the product to the nearest authorized service center. This is the fastest option for most repairs.
- **Doorstep Pickup:** Our logistics partner picks up the product from your address, it is repaired at the service center, and returned to you. Available in most major cities.
- **Onsite Service:** A certified technician visits your location for diagnosis and repair. Available for large appliances, commercial equipment, and select premium products.

Service mode availability depends on your product category and pin code. Enter your details during claim submission to see available options.

**Q: How do I find an authorized service center near me?**  
A: Go to our website and navigate to Support → Service Center Locator. Enter your city, pin code, or state to find the nearest authorized service center along with their address, working hours, and contact number. You can also call our support helpline and ask for the nearest service center in your area.

**Q: Do I have to pay anything for warranty service?**  
A: No. Repairs, parts replacement, and labor costs are fully covered under a valid warranty at no charge to you. The only potential costs are:

- **Shipping charges** for doorstep pickup in remote or non-serviceable areas (notified at the time of claim)
- **Charges for damage not covered** under warranty if additional non-warranty issues are found during service (you will be informed and asked to approve before any chargeable work is done)

You will never be charged without prior notice and consent.

**Q: Will my data be erased during warranty repair?**  
A: In many cases, especially for electronics, data may be erased during diagnostic or repair procedures. We strongly recommend **backing up all personal data** before submitting a device for warranty service. We are not responsible for any data loss during the repair process. If the repair involves a factory reset or storage component replacement, all data on the device will be permanently lost.

**Q: Can I request a temporary replacement device while mine is being repaired?**  
A: Loaner devices are available for select premium product categories (such as laptops and high-end smartphones) during the warranty repair period. This service is available in major metros and must be requested at the time of claim submission. A refundable security deposit may be required for the loaner device. Check during claim submission whether this option is available for your product and location.

**Q: What warranty does a repaired or replaced product carry?**  
A: A product repaired under warranty carries the remainder of the original warranty period or 90 days from the date of repair completion, whichever is longer. A replacement unit carries a fresh warranty period equivalent to the standard warranty of a new product of the same model. Replacement warranty details will be provided in writing when the replacement is dispatched.

## SECTION 6 — EXTENDED WARRANTY & PROTECTION PLANS
---

**Q: What is an extended warranty and how is it different from the standard warranty?**  
A: The standard warranty is included with the product at no extra cost and covers manufacturing defects for a defined period. An extended warranty is an optional, purchasable plan that extends coverage beyond the standard period and often includes additional protections such as accidental damage coverage, screen damage, and priority service. Think of the extended warranty as a comprehensive service plan layered on top of the standard warranty.

**Q: How do I purchase an extended warranty?**  
A: Extended warranties can be purchased:

- **At the time of purchase:** Add the plan to your cart on the product page or at checkout.
- **Within 6 months of purchase:** Go to My Products → Select product → Buy Extended Warranty. Extended warranty plans purchased after delivery are available until 6 months from the purchase date.

Extended warranties purchased after the standard warranty expires are generally not accepted. Plans are product-specific and non-transferable.

**Q: What does the extended warranty cover that the standard warranty does not?**  
A: Extended warranty plans typically add the following on top of standard coverage:

- Accidental damage from drops and impacts
- Accidental liquid spills (not submersion)
- Cracked or broken screens
- Electrical surge damage
- Priority claim processing (faster turnaround)
- Free onsite service visits (where applicable)
- Annual preventive maintenance checks (for appliances)

Exact coverage depends on the plan tier (Basic, Standard, or Comprehensive). Review the plan brochure before purchase.

**Q: Can I transfer the extended warranty if I sell my product?**  
A: Standard extended warranties are non-transferable and remain linked to the original purchaser's account. However, some premium protection plans offer a one-time transfer option. To check if your plan supports transfer, go to My Products → Extended Warranty → Transfer Options. A nominal transfer fee may apply, and the transfer must be completed through the official portal.

**Q: How do I cancel my extended warranty plan and get a refund?**  
A: You can cancel an extended warranty plan within 30 days of purchase for a full refund, provided no claims have been made under the plan. After 30 days, a prorated refund may be issued based on the unused warranty period minus a cancellation fee. To cancel, go to My Products → Extended Warranty → Cancel Plan, or contact billing support. Once a claim has been made, the plan cannot be cancelled for a refund.

**Q: Does the extended warranty cover pre-existing conditions?**  
A: No. Extended warranty plans do not cover defects or damage that existed before the plan was purchased or activated. Pre-existing conditions are assessed at the time of first claim. If a defect is found to have pre-dated the plan start date, the claim will be denied. This is why it is important to inspect the product thoroughly and purchase the plan while the product is in good working condition, ideally at the time of purchase.

## SECTION 7 — WARRANTY FOR SPECIFIC PRODUCT CATEGORIES
---

**Q: What is the warranty policy for smartphones and tablets?**  
A: Smartphones and tablets come with a 1-year standard warranty covering manufacturing defects in hardware and pre-installed software. The battery is covered for 6 months or 500 charge cycles, whichever comes first. Screen defects exceeding 5 dead pixels in the center zone are covered. Physical damage, liquid damage, and unauthorized software modifications void the warranty. An extended warranty with accidental damage protection is available for up to 2 additional years.

**Q: What is the warranty policy for laptops and computers?**  
A: Laptops and desktop computers carry a 1-year standard warranty covering hardware defects, including the display, keyboard, motherboard, and storage. RAM and storage upgrades performed by authorized centers do not void the warranty. The battery is covered for 1 year or up to 300 charge cycles. Software issues caused by the user (OS reinstall, virus) are not covered. Extended warranty plans of up to 3 years are available.

**Q: What is the warranty policy for home appliances?**  
A: Home appliances such as refrigerators, washing machines, air conditioners, and microwaves typically carry:

- **1-year comprehensive warranty** on the full product
- **3–5 year warranty on the compressor** (for refrigerators and ACs)
- **5-year warranty on the motor** (for washing machines)

Installation defects are covered if installation was performed by our authorized team. Damage from voltage fluctuations is not covered unless a voltage stabilizer is in use as recommended. Annual maintenance contracts (AMC) are available after the warranty period.

**Q: What is the warranty policy for wearables and smart devices?**  
A: Wearables (smartwatches, fitness bands) and smart home devices (smart speakers, cameras) come with a 1-year standard warranty. Strap bands, screen protectors, and charging contacts are consumables and carry a 3-month warranty. Water resistance is covered only within the stated IP rating. Battery degradation below 80% capacity within the warranty period qualifies for replacement. Extended warranties are available at checkout.

**Q: What is the warranty policy for furniture and home furnishings?**  
A: Furniture products carry the following warranties:

- **Structural warranty:** 2–5 years against frame cracks, joint failures, and structural collapse under normal use
- **Fabric/upholstery:** 1 year against stitching defects, abnormal fading, or peeling
- **Hardware (hinges, sliders, locks):** 1 year

Warranty does not cover damage from misuse, excessive weight beyond the rated capacity, exposure to direct sunlight causing fading, or pest damage. Warranty service for furniture is onsite only — do not attempt to ship furniture for warranty service.

**Q: What is the warranty for industrial or commercial equipment?**  
A: Industrial and commercial equipment warranties are governed by the product-specific contract signed at purchase. In general:

- **Standard coverage:** 1–2 years on parts and labor
- **Onsite service:** Included for equipment above a specified purchase value
- **Preventive maintenance:** Quarterly visits included in some enterprise plans
- **Parts availability:** Guaranteed for a minimum of 5 years after discontinuation

Warranty for commercial equipment requires registration within 30 days of installation. Contact your account manager or the B2B support team for specific terms related to your equipment.

## SECTION 8 — INTERNATIONAL & CROSS-BORDER WARRANTY
---

**Q: Is my product warranty valid internationally?**  
A: Warranty validity outside the country of purchase depends on the product type:

- **International warranty products:** Certain products (listed on the product page) carry a global warranty honored in all countries where we have authorized service centers.
- **Regional warranty products:** Most standard products carry regional warranties valid only in the country of purchase.

If you are traveling internationally with a product under regional warranty and need service, contact our international support team. Service may be arranged at an additional charge or by shipping the product back to the country of purchase.

**Q: I purchased a product abroad. Can I claim warranty in my home country?**  
A: If the product carries an international or global warranty, yes — you can claim warranty in your home country through our local authorized service center. If the product carries a regional warranty tied to the country of purchase, the home country service center may still assist but may charge for service or parts. Bring the original purchase invoice and check the warranty card for the warranty region designation.

**Q: Does buying from an unauthorized reseller affect my warranty?**  
A: Yes. Products purchased from unauthorized or grey market sellers may not be eligible for manufacturer warranty service. Warranty coverage is valid only for products purchased through authorized retailers, official websites, or certified channel partners. If you are unsure whether a seller is authorized, check the Authorized Reseller List on our website before purchasing. Products identified as grey imports will have warranty claims denied.

## SECTION 9 — ESCALATION & DISPUTE RESOLUTION
---

**Q: What should I do if I am not satisfied with the warranty service outcome?**  
A: If you are dissatisfied with the resolution provided, follow this escalation path:

1. **Level 1 — Re-open the claim:** Contact warranty support and request a review of the claim, providing additional details or evidence.
2. **Level 2 — Escalate to the Service Manager:** Request escalation to the regional service manager via our support portal.
3. **Level 3 — Submit a formal complaint:** Use the Grievance Redressal form under Support → Complaints. A senior resolution specialist will respond within 5 business days.
4. **Level 4 — Consumer Forum:** If the issue remains unresolved, you may escalate to the appropriate consumer protection forum or regulatory body in your region.

**Q: What are my consumer rights regarding product warranty?**  
A: Consumer protection laws in most jurisdictions provide the following rights regarding product warranties:

- The right to a product that is fit for purpose and of satisfactory quality
- The right to repair, replacement, or refund for defective products
- The right to warranty service without excessive delay
- Protection against misleading warranty terms or exclusions that are unfair or illegal

In India, the Consumer Protection Act 2019 governs these rights. In the EU, the Sale of Goods Directive provides a minimum 2-year statutory guarantee. In the US, the Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act governs written warranties. You may also contact your country's consumer affairs authority if your rights are not honored.

**Q: How do I file a formal complaint against warranty service?**  
A: To file a formal complaint:

1. Log in to your account and go to Support → Complaints → File New Complaint.
2. Provide your claim reference number, description of the issue, and the resolution you are seeking.
3. Attach any supporting evidence (photos, emails, service reports).
4. Submit the complaint. A senior resolution specialist will contact you within 5 business days.

Alternatively, you may send a written complaint to our official grievance officer address listed in the Terms of Service. All formal complaints are acknowledged within 48 hours.

## SECTION 10 — FREQUENTLY ENCOUNTERED SCENARIOS
---

**Q: My product stopped working within a week of purchase. What should I do?**  
A: A product that stops working within the first 7 days of purchase is treated as a Dead on Arrival (DOA) case. DOA products are eligible for an immediate replacement without going through the standard repair process. Contact customer support within 7 days of delivery and raise a DOA claim. Provide unboxing photos or video if available, as this helps expedite the process.

**Q: My product has the same defect recurring after repair. What are my options?**  
A: If the same defect recurs after a warranty repair, it is treated as a persistent fault. After the second repair attempt for the same defect within the warranty period, you are eligible to request a product replacement instead of another repair. Document each repair with the job card or repair report provided by the service center. Submit a replacement request via the claim portal referencing both job card numbers.

**Q: I received a refurbished or open-box product. Does it carry warranty?**  
A: Yes. Certified refurbished and open-box products sold through our official channels carry a warranty, typically shorter than that of a new product:

- **Certified Refurbished:** 6 months to 1 year warranty
- **Open-box:** 6-month warranty

The exact warranty period is mentioned on the product listing and the accompanying documentation. Refurbished products are not eligible for the same extended warranty plans as new products, though some protection plans may be available.

**Q: The product was damaged during delivery. Is this covered under warranty?**  
A: Damage that occurs during shipping or delivery is covered under our Delivery Damage Policy, which is separate from the product warranty. If you receive a visibly damaged package, refuse the delivery or note the damage with the delivery agent. Report the issue to customer support within 48 hours of delivery with photos of the packaging and the product. A replacement or refund will be issued. Delivery damage reported after 48 hours may not be accepted.

**Q: I bought the product as a gift. Can the recipient claim warranty?**  
A: Yes. The gift recipient can claim warranty using the original purchase invoice. The warranty is tied to the product (via serial number) and not the buyer's account. The recipient should register the product in their own account for easier claim processing. If the purchase invoice mentions a gifting note, it helps service centers process the claim without friction.

**Q: What should I do if a spare part needed for my repair is out of stock?**  
A: If the required spare part is temporarily out of stock, our service center will provide you with a written estimate of the expected availability date. If the part is unavailable for more than 30 days from the date your claim was approved, you are entitled to request a product replacement of the same or equivalent model. If no equivalent model is available, a refund at the current market value may be offered.
