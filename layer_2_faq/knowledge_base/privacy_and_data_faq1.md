## SECTION 1 — PRIVACY POLICY BASICS
---

**Q: What is your privacy policy?**  
A: Our Privacy Policy is a legal document that explains what personal data we collect from users, how we use it, who we share it with, how long we keep it, and what rights you have over your data. It is written in plain language and is publicly accessible on our website under the "Privacy Policy" link in the footer. We recommend reading it when you create an account and reviewing it whenever it is updated, as we notify users of significant changes via email.

**Q: Has your privacy policy changed recently?**  
A: We update our Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, new features, or regulatory requirements. Whenever a material change is made, we notify registered users via email at least 30 days before the change takes effect. A version history of the Privacy Policy is maintained on our website so you can see exactly what changed and when. The current effective date is displayed at the top of the Privacy Policy page.

**Q: Which privacy laws and regulations do you comply with?**  
A: We comply with applicable data protection laws across all regions where we operate. Key regulations include:

- **GDPR** (General Data Protection Regulation) — European Union and UK
- **CCPA / CPRA** (California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act) — California, USA
- **PDPB / DPDP Act** (Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023) — India
- **PIPEDA** (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act) — Canada
- **LGPD** (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados) — Brazil
- **POPIA** (Protection of Personal Information Act) — South Africa

If you are located in a region with specific local data protection laws, those laws' rights and obligations apply to your data in addition to our general policy.

**Q: Who is responsible for protecting my data within your organization?**  
A: Our Data Protection Officer (DPO) is responsible for overseeing data protection strategy and compliance within our organization. The DPO ensures that we handle personal data in line with applicable laws and our Privacy Policy. You can contact the DPO directly for privacy-related concerns at our designated privacy email address listed in the Privacy Policy. For enterprise customers, a dedicated privacy point of contact is also available.

**Q: Do you have a dedicated privacy team?**  
A: Yes. We have an internal Privacy & Security team responsible for implementing privacy controls, conducting data protection impact assessments (DPIAs), managing data subject requests, handling breach notifications, and training staff on data privacy best practices. The team works alongside legal, engineering, and product teams to build privacy into our systems by design (privacy by design).

## SECTION 2 — DATA WE COLLECT
---

**Q: What personal data do you collect from me?**  
A: We collect the following categories of personal data depending on how you use our platform:

- **Identity data:** Full name, username, date of birth, gender
- **Contact data:** Email address, phone number, mailing address
- **Account data:** Login credentials (password stored as a hash), account preferences
- **Payment data:** Card type, last 4 digits, billing address (full card details handled by PCI-compliant processor)
- **Usage data:** Features accessed, pages visited, time spent, actions taken on the platform
- **Device & technical data:** IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers
- **Communication data:** Support tickets, chat transcripts, feedback submitted
- **Location data:** Approximate location derived from IP (precise GPS only if explicitly permitted)

We collect only what is necessary to provide, improve, and secure our services.

**Q: Do you collect data from children?**  
A: No. Our services are not directed at children under the age of 13 (or 16 in the EU under GDPR). We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If we discover that a minor has created an account without parental consent, the account will be deleted along with all associated data. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has registered, contact our privacy team immediately for account removal.

**Q: Do you collect my location data?**  
A: We collect approximate location data derived from your IP address for security purposes (e.g., detecting unusual login locations) and to provide region-specific features. We do not collect precise GPS-level location data unless you explicitly grant location permission in our mobile app for a specific feature (e.g., finding nearby service centers). You can revoke location permission at any time in your device settings or app permissions.

**Q: Do you track my behavior on the platform?**  
A: Yes. We collect usage and behavioral data to understand how users interact with our platform. This helps us improve features, personalize your experience, and fix issues. Behavioral data includes pages visited, features used, buttons clicked, session duration, and error logs. This data is collected via cookies, session logs, and analytics tools. You can manage cookie preferences through the Cookie Settings panel accessible from the website footer.

**Q: What data do you collect during customer support interactions?**  
A: When you contact customer support, we collect and retain the content of your messages (chat transcripts, emails, call recordings if applicable), your account information used to verify identity, the nature of your issue and resolution provided, and any attachments or screenshots you submit. This data is used to resolve your issue and improve support quality. Support records are retained for up to 3 years for quality and compliance purposes.

**Q: Do you collect data when I am not logged in?**  
A: Yes. Even when you are not logged in, we collect non-personal and aggregated data such as browser type, pages visited, time spent on pages, and general geographic region (from IP address). This helps us understand how visitors discover and navigate our website. This data is not linked to a personal account unless you subsequently log in during the same session.

## SECTION 3 — HOW WE USE YOUR DATA
---

**Q: How do you use my personal data?**  
A: We use your personal data for the following purposes:

- **Service delivery:** Creating and managing your account, processing orders, providing features
- **Communication:** Sending transactional emails (receipts, alerts), responding to support queries
- **Personalization:** Customizing your experience based on preferences and usage history
- **Security:** Detecting fraud, preventing unauthorized access, verifying identity
- **Analytics & improvement:** Understanding platform usage to improve features and fix bugs
- **Legal compliance:** Meeting our obligations under applicable laws and regulations
- **Marketing (with consent):** Sending promotional emails and offers (only if you opted in)

We do not use your data for any purpose not described in our Privacy Policy without your consent.

**Q: Do you use my data for personalized advertising?**  
A: We do not sell your data to third-party advertisers. However, if you have opted in to marketing communications, we may use your data (such as purchase history and usage patterns) to personalize recommendations and promotional content shown within our platform. We do not use your data to serve ads on third-party websites. You can opt out of personalized communications at any time through Account Settings → Notifications → Marketing Preferences.

**Q: Do you use my data to train AI or machine learning models?**  
A: We may use anonymized and aggregated usage data to train and improve internal models that enhance platform features such as search, recommendations, and support automation. We do not use personally identifiable information (PII) to train AI models without explicit consent. We do not share your data with third-party AI companies for training purposes. If you have concerns about AI data usage, contact our privacy team.

**Q: What is the legal basis for processing my personal data?**  
A: Under GDPR and similar regulations, every data processing activity must have a lawful basis. We process your data under the following legal bases:

- **Contractual necessity:** Processing required to deliver the services you signed up for (e.g., account management, order processing)
- **Legitimate interests:** Processing for security, fraud prevention, and product improvement where our interests do not override your rights
- **Legal obligation:** Processing required to comply with applicable laws (e.g., tax records, law enforcement requests)
- **Consent:** Processing for optional activities such as marketing emails and cookie-based analytics, where you have explicitly opted in

You can withdraw consent for consent-based processing at any time.

## SECTION 4 — DATA SHARING & THIRD PARTIES
---

**Q: Do you sell my personal data to third parties?**  
A: No. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to any third party for their own commercial use. This includes advertising networks, data brokers, and analytics companies that would use your data for their own profit. Under the CCPA, you have the explicit right to opt out of the sale of personal information — and as a matter of policy, we do not engage in such sales regardless of your location.

**Q: Who do you share my data with?**  
A: We share your data only with trusted parties where necessary to operate our services:

- **Service providers:** Cloud hosting, payment processors, email delivery, customer support tools (under strict data processing agreements)
- **Analytics partners:** Anonymized or aggregated data for platform analytics (e.g., Google Analytics with IP anonymization)
- **Legal authorities:** When required by law, court order, or regulatory request
- **Business transfers:** In the event of a merger, acquisition, or asset sale (you will be notified)
- **With your consent:** Any other sharing will only happen with your explicit permission

All third-party providers are bound by data processing agreements (DPAs) that restrict them from using your data for any purpose beyond the service they provide to us.

**Q: Which third-party tools or services do you use that handle my data?**  
A: We use carefully vetted third-party tools across the following categories:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Cloud Infrastructure | AWS, Google Cloud |
| Payment Processing | Stripe, Razorpay |
| Email Delivery | SendGrid, Amazon SES |
| Customer Support | Zendesk, Intercom |
| Analytics | Google Analytics, Mixpanel |
| Error Monitoring | Sentry |
| Security / Fraud Detection | Internal tools + third-party signals |

Each of these vendors is assessed for privacy and security compliance. A full list of sub-processors is available upon request from our privacy team.

**Q: Is my data transferred to other countries?**  
A: Yes. As we use cloud infrastructure and global service providers, your data may be transferred to and stored in countries outside your home country, including the United States, European Union member states, and other locations. For such transfers, we ensure adequate safeguards are in place, such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for EU data, or equivalent mechanisms required by applicable laws. Details of cross-border transfer safeguards are in our Privacy Policy.

**Q: Can I request that my data not be shared with third parties?**  
A: You can limit certain types of data sharing. Specifically, you can opt out of data sharing used for analytics and marketing by adjusting your Cookie Settings and Marketing Preferences in Account Settings. However, some data sharing is essential for delivering our services — for example, sharing payment data with our payment processor. These essential transfers cannot be opted out of without discontinuing use of the service. Contact our privacy team for detailed guidance on your specific situation.

**Q: What happens to my data if you are acquired by another company?**  
A: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or part of our assets, your personal data may be transferred to the acquiring entity as part of the business transaction. We will notify you via email and a prominent notice on our website at least 30 days before such a transfer occurs. The acquiring company will be required to honor the commitments made in our Privacy Policy. You will have the option to delete your account before the transfer is finalized if you do not agree to the new terms.

## SECTION 5 — COOKIES & TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES
---

**Q: What are cookies and why do you use them?**  
A: Cookies are small text files stored on your device by a website. We use cookies for the following purposes:

- **Essential cookies:** Required for basic site functionality such as keeping you logged in and remembering cart contents. Cannot be disabled.
- **Preference cookies:** Store your settings and preferences (language, theme, etc.)
- **Analytics cookies:** Help us understand how visitors use our site, using anonymized data
- **Marketing cookies:** Used to track visits and measure effectiveness of our own campaigns (not third-party ad networks)

A full list of cookies we use, their purpose, and retention periods is available in our Cookie Policy, accessible from the website footer.

**Q: How do I manage or disable cookies?**  
A: You can manage cookie preferences in two ways:

1. **Cookie Settings panel:** Click "Cookie Settings" in the website footer. You can toggle analytics and preference cookies on or off. Essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are required for the site to function.
2. **Browser settings:** Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies entirely via browser privacy settings. Note that blocking all cookies may affect platform functionality.

Your cookie preferences are saved on your device and will be remembered for future visits unless you clear your browser's cookies and site data.

**Q: Do you use tracking pixels or web beacons?**  
A: Yes. Our marketing emails may include a tracking pixel — a tiny, invisible image — that tells us when the email was opened and from what type of device. This helps us measure email campaign effectiveness. To disable email tracking, you can either set your email client to block remote images or opt out of marketing emails entirely from Account Settings → Notifications → Email Preferences. We do not use tracking pixels to collect personally identifiable information beyond open and click events.

**Q: Do you use fingerprinting or device tracking?**  
A: We use device signals such as browser type, operating system, screen resolution, and installed fonts as part of our fraud detection and security systems to identify suspicious login activity. This is distinct from advertising-oriented fingerprinting. We do not use device fingerprinting for advertising purposes or to track your behavior across other websites. This security-related device recognition cannot be opted out of, as it protects the integrity of your account.

## SECTION 6 — YOUR PRIVACY RIGHTS
---

**Q: What privacy rights do I have over my personal data?**  
A: Depending on your region, you have some or all of the following rights:

- **Right to Access:** Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you
- **Right to Rectification:** Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
- **Right to Erasure:** Request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten")
- **Right to Restriction:** Ask us to stop processing your data in certain ways
- **Right to Portability:** Receive your data in a machine-readable format (e.g., JSON, CSV)
- **Right to Object:** Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing
- **Right to Withdraw Consent:** Withdraw consent for any consent-based processing at any time
- **Right Not to Be Subject to Automated Decisions:** Request human review of automated decisions that significantly affect you

To exercise any of these rights, visit Account Settings → Privacy or contact our privacy team.

**Q: How do I request a copy of all data you hold about me?**  
A: To submit a Data Subject Access Request (DSAR):

1. Log in and go to Account Settings → Privacy → Request My Data.
2. Select the data categories you want included.
3. Verify your identity (you may be asked to confirm via email or provide ID for high-sensitivity requests).
4. Submit the request.

We will provide your data in a structured, machine-readable format (JSON or CSV) within 30 days of verified receipt of your request. The export will include profile data, order history, support communications, and activity logs associated with your account.

**Q: How do I correct inaccurate personal data?**  
A: You can directly update most of your personal data from Account Settings → Profile. This includes your name, phone number, address, and communication preferences. For data that cannot be changed from your profile (such as billing records or data in archived records), submit a Rectification Request via Account Settings → Privacy → Correct My Data, explaining the inaccuracy. We will review and update within 14 days.

**Q: How do I request deletion of my personal data?**  
A: To request data deletion (erasure):

1. Go to Account Settings → Privacy → Request Data Deletion.
2. Specify whether you want to delete only your account or request full erasure of all associated data.
3. Confirm your identity.
4. Submit the request.

We will process the deletion within 30 days. Note that certain data is exempt from deletion due to legal obligations — for example, transaction records must be retained for up to 7 years for tax and financial compliance. You will receive a confirmation email listing what was deleted and what was retained with the legal basis for retention.

**Q: Can I download my data and take it to another service?**  
A: Yes. The right to data portability allows you to receive your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format. Go to Account Settings → Privacy → Export My Data. You can download your profile information, order history, saved preferences, and activity data in JSON or CSV format. This data can then be imported into compatible third-party services. The export is available within 30 days of the request.

**Q: How do I opt out of marketing communications?**  
A: You can opt out of marketing emails, SMS, and push notifications in two ways:

1. **From your account:** Go to Account Settings → Notifications → Marketing Preferences and toggle off the types of communication you no longer wish to receive.
2. **From the email itself:** Every marketing email contains an "Unsubscribe" link at the bottom. Clicking it will unsubscribe you from that mailing list within 10 business days.

Opting out of marketing does not affect transactional communications such as order confirmations, password resets, and security alerts — those are essential and cannot be disabled.

**Q: How do I withdraw consent for data processing?**  
A: For any data processing activity that relies on your consent (such as marketing emails, analytics cookies, or optional data features), you can withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing. To withdraw consent, go to Account Settings → Privacy → Manage Consents and toggle off the relevant activity. Withdrawal takes effect immediately. If you are unsure what consents you have given, the Manage Consents page provides a full log of your consent history.

**Q: How long does it take to process a privacy request?**  
A: We aim to respond to all privacy requests within the following timelines:

| Request Type | Response Time |
|---|---|
| Data Access (DSAR) | Within 30 days |
| Data Deletion | Within 30 days |
| Data Correction | Within 14 days |
| Consent Withdrawal | Immediately |
| Data Portability Export | Within 30 days |
| Opt-out of Marketing | Within 10 business days |

If a request is complex or we receive a high volume of requests, we may extend the deadline by an additional 30 days and will notify you of the extension.

## SECTION 7 — DATA STORAGE & RETENTION
---

**Q: Where is my data stored?**  
A: Your data is stored in secure, industry-standard cloud data centers. Primary data storage is in servers located in the region closest to your country of residence to comply with data localization preferences. For Indian users, primary data is stored in data centers within India. For EU users, primary data is stored within the European Economic Area (EEA). Backup copies may be maintained in geographically separate locations for disaster recovery purposes.

**Q: How long do you keep my personal data?**  
A: Retention periods vary by data category:

| Data Category | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Account & profile data | Until account deletion + 30 days |
| Transaction & billing records | 7 years (tax compliance) |
| Support tickets & communications | 3 years |
| Usage & analytics logs | 13 months (rolling) |
| Security & access logs | 12 months |
| Marketing consent records | Until opt-out + 5 years |
| Backup copies | Up to 90 days after deletion request |

After the applicable retention period, data is permanently and securely deleted or anonymized so it can no longer be linked to you.

**Q: What happens to my data after I delete my account?**  
A: When you delete your account, the following happens:

- Your profile and account data are immediately deactivated and hidden.
- Most personal data is deleted within 30 days.
- Transaction records are retained for up to 7 years as required by tax law.
- Support communications are retained for up to 3 years.
- Backup copies containing your data are purged within 90 days of the deletion request.
- Anonymized, aggregated data that cannot be linked back to you may be retained indefinitely for analytics.

You will receive an email confirming the deletion and listing any data retained with the legal reason for retention.

**Q: How is my data protected from loss or corruption?**  
A: We implement multiple layers of data protection to prevent loss or corruption:

- **Automated backups:** Data is backed up daily with redundant copies stored in geographically separate locations.
- **Replication:** Critical databases are replicated in real time across multiple availability zones.
- **Versioning:** Key data is version-controlled so accidental changes can be rolled back.
- **Disaster recovery:** We maintain a tested disaster recovery plan with a defined Recovery Point Objective (RPO) of less than 1 hour.
- **Data integrity checks:** Automated checks run continuously to detect and flag data corruption.

## SECTION 8 — DATA SECURITY
---

**Q: How do you secure my personal data?**  
A: We implement a comprehensive set of technical and organizational security measures, including:

- **Encryption in transit:** All data transmitted between your device and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.
- **Encryption at rest:** Sensitive data stored in our databases is encrypted using AES-256.
- **Access control:** Personal data is accessible only to employees who need it for their job. Role-based access controls (RBAC) and least-privilege principles are enforced.
- **Penetration testing:** We conduct regular third-party security audits and penetration tests.
- **Security certifications:** We maintain ISO 27001 and SOC 2 Type II certifications.
- **Vulnerability management:** Security patches are applied promptly and a responsible disclosure program is in place.

**Q: What happens if there is a data breach?**  
A: In the event of a data breach that poses a risk to your personal data, we will:

1. **Contain the breach** immediately upon detection using our incident response procedures.
2. **Assess the impact** to determine what data was affected and who is at risk.
3. **Notify regulatory authorities** within 72 hours as required under GDPR and other applicable laws.
4. **Notify affected users** via email within 72 hours if the breach is likely to result in high risk to your rights and freedoms.
5. **Provide guidance** on steps you should take to protect yourself (e.g., change your password, monitor your accounts).
6. **Conduct a post-incident review** and implement measures to prevent recurrence.

We maintain a detailed incident response plan and conduct regular drills to ensure readiness.

**Q: How do I report a security vulnerability or suspected data breach?**  
A: If you discover a security vulnerability or suspect a data breach involving your account or our platform, please contact our security team immediately at our dedicated security disclosure email address listed in our Security Policy page. For account-level issues (e.g., unauthorized access to your account), contact customer support and our security team will be engaged. Do not disclose vulnerabilities publicly before we have had an opportunity to investigate and remediate — responsible disclosure is appreciated and we may recognize your contribution.

**Q: Do your employees have access to my personal data?**  
A: Access to personal data is granted on a strict need-to-know basis. Only employees in specific roles — such as customer support agents, security analysts, and compliance officers — have access to user data, and only to the extent required for their job function. All such access is logged and audited. Employees are bound by confidentiality agreements and undergo regular privacy and security training. Access permissions are reviewed quarterly and revoked promptly when no longer needed.

## SECTION 9 — SPECIAL CATEGORIES OF DATA
---

**Q: Do you collect sensitive personal data such as health or financial information?**  
A: We do not intentionally collect special category data such as health information, biometric data, racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, political opinions, or criminal records. If you voluntarily share such information in a support ticket or other communication, it is handled with heightened care and deleted once the purpose is fulfilled. If you are using a specialized product variant that involves health or financial data, a separate addendum to the Privacy Policy governs those data types.

**Q: How is my payment information protected?**  
A: We do not store your full credit or debit card details on our servers. All payment information is processed and stored by our PCI-DSS Level 1 certified payment processor. We retain only non-sensitive payment identifiers such as the card type, last four digits, and expiry month/year for display purposes in your billing section. In the event of a breach of our systems, your full card details would not be exposed as we do not have them.

**Q: Do you store my passwords?**  
A: No. We never store your password in plain text. Passwords are processed through a one-way cryptographic hashing algorithm (bcrypt with a strong salt) before being stored. This means even our internal staff cannot retrieve your original password. If we suspect a breach, we will proactively prompt you to reset your password. We recommend using a strong, unique password and enabling two-factor authentication for maximum account security.

## SECTION 10 — CHILDREN'S PRIVACY
---

**Q: What is your policy on collecting data from children under 13?**  
A: We strictly do not knowingly collect or process personal data from children under the age of 13. Our platform is designed for adults and users above the minimum age threshold. We use age-gate mechanisms during registration to screen out users below the minimum age. If we learn that data from a minor under 13 has been collected, it is deleted immediately.

**Q: What age is required to create an account?**  
A: Users must be at least 18 years old to create an account independently. In some regions, users between 13 and 17 may use the platform with verified parental consent. Under GDPR, the minimum age for consent to data processing is 16 (or lower if national law permits, but not below 13). Parental consent features are available for educational or family-oriented product variants. Contact support for guidance on setting up a supervised minor account where supported.

## SECTION 11 — AUTOMATED DECISIONS & PROFILING
---

**Q: Do you make automated decisions about me?**  
A: Yes, in limited cases. We use automated systems for fraud detection (flagging suspicious login attempts or transactions), spam filtering in support communications, and personalizing content recommendations. These automated processes do not produce decisions with significant legal or similarly significant effects without a human review layer. If an automated decision does significantly affect you (e.g., an account suspension triggered by fraud detection), you have the right to request human review.

**Q: How do I request a human review of an automated decision?**  
A: If you believe an automated decision has unfairly affected your account or experience, submit a Human Review Request via Account Settings → Privacy → Request Human Review or contact our support team. Describe the decision in question and why you believe it was incorrect. A human reviewer will assess your case and respond within 10 business days. During the review period, any account restriction triggered by the automated decision may be temporarily lifted at the reviewer's discretion.

## SECTION 12 — LAW ENFORCEMENT & LEGAL REQUESTS
---

**Q: Do you share my data with government or law enforcement agencies?**  
A: We disclose user data to government or law enforcement agencies only when legally required to do so — for example, in response to a valid court order, subpoena, search warrant, or equivalent legal process. We review every such request for legal validity before complying. Where legally permitted, we notify the affected user before disclosing their data. We do not provide voluntary access to law enforcement without a legal mandate.

**Q: How do you handle legal requests for user data?**  
A: Our legal team reviews each request to verify its authenticity, jurisdiction, and legal sufficiency. Overly broad or legally deficient requests are challenged or rejected. We comply only with the minimum data required to satisfy the specific legal request. A transparency report documenting the volume and types of legal requests we receive is published annually on our website.

## SECTION 13 — PRIVACY FOR BUSINESS & ENTERPRISE USERS
---

**Q: How does data privacy work for enterprise or business accounts?**  
A: For enterprise accounts, we enter into a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) that governs how we process personal data on your organization's behalf. In this context, your organization is the data controller and we act as the data processor. The DPA outlines the scope of processing, security measures, sub-processor disclosures, and procedures for handling data subject requests from your end users. Enterprise DPAs are available upon request from our sales or legal team.

**Q: Can I get a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) signed?**  
A: Yes. For business customers processing personal data of EU or UK residents or operating under other regulated environments, a signed DPA is available. Contact our legal or sales team to initiate the DPA process. The DPA incorporates Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for international transfers where applicable. For enterprise contracts, the DPA is typically included as an addendum to the Master Service Agreement (MSA).

**Q: Do you offer data residency options for enterprise customers?**  
A: Yes. Enterprise customers can request data residency in a specific geographic region (e.g., EU-only, India-only, US-only). Data residency ensures that your organization's primary data is stored and processed only within the designated region. This is available as a configurable option on select enterprise plans. Additional charges may apply. Contact your account manager or enterprise sales team to discuss data residency requirements and availability for your region.
